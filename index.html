How To Play

(i) TENIZ a game inspired by WORDLE, tests your knowledge on Tennis Grand Slam Champions of the Open Era. The objective is to identify the Champion Tennis Player every day, using the Least Number Of Clues.

(ii) Use the search area to key in your answer and hit Enter . A new clue will be revelaed after each INCORRECT guess.

(iii) Hints include Year won, Slam won, Country, Gender, Total GS Singles Titles and Style of Play (RH vs LH). If a person has won mutliple Slams the reference will be for just 1 of their wins. The same player can be repeated on another day with another of their wins. Clues are revealed after each guess, as shown below.

(iv) After each wrong guess, you get secondary clues, to show how the player you entered matched against the original clues (shown in same order of original clues even if they are not yet revealed). 🟢 Indicates a match and 🔴 indicates a no-match.

(v) For eg: a 🟢 in the first position (year), indicates that the person you entered has won a GS in the same year as in question.

(vi) Similarly, a 🔴 in the second position (Slam), indicates that the person you entered has not won this particular slam in question. Secondary clues might help you get to the player sooner, as you might get to know what a main clue is before they are revealed.
Legend :AO - Australian Open | FO - French Open | WIM - Wimbledon | USO - US Open | GS - Grand Slam | RH - Right Handed | LH - Left Handed
