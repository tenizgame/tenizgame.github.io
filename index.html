How To Play

(i) TENIZ, a game inspired by WORDLE, tests your knowledge on Tennis Grand Slam Champions of the Open Era. The objective is to identify the Champion Tennis Player every day, using the Least Number Of Clues.

(ii) Use the search area to key in your answer and hit Enter . In the default (normal) mode, after each guess, you get clues to show how the player you entered, matched against the attributes of the player in question.

(iii) Attributes include Year won, Slam won, Country, Gender, Total GS Singles Titles and Style of Play (RH vs LH). If a person has won mutliple Slams the reference will be for just 1 of their wins. The same player can be repeated on another day with another of their wins.

(iv) 🟢 Indicates a match, 🔴 indicates a no-match and 🟡 indicates that what you guessed is within a range of +/- 3 (valid for year and titles only). 🟡 for Country, indicates a match in the Continent.

(v) For eg: a 🟢 in the first position (year), indicates that the person you entered has won a GS in the same year as in question. Similarly, a 🔴 in the second position (Slam), indicates that the person you entered has not won this particular slam in question.

(vi) You also have an option to switch to the Easy Mode any time during the game. In the Easy Mode the attributes are revealed, as shown below, after each INCORRECT guess.

Legend :AO - Australian Open | FO - French Open | WIM - Wimbledon | USO - US Open | GS - Grand Slam | RH - Right Handed | LH - Left Handed
