How To Play

(i) TENIZ, a game inspired by WORDLE, tests your knowledge on Tennis Grand Slam Champions of the Open Era. The objective is to identify the Champion Tennis Player every day, using the Clues. Check out a Video I made about the game here, and then read the rest of this page to learn more.

(ii) The mystery player needs to be identified based on attributes (shown at the top of the page) of ONE OF THEIR GS WINS. These attributes include Year won, Slam won, Country (at the time of win), Gender, Total GS Singles Titles and Style of Play (RH vs LH). If a person has won mutliple Slams, the same player can be repeated on another day with another of their Wins.

(iii) Use the search area to key in your answer and hit Enter . After each guess, details of the player you entererd are listed. You also get INDICATORS to show how the attributes of the player you entered, match against that of the player in question.

(iv) 🟢 Indicates a match, 🔴 indicates a no-match and 🟡 indicates that what you guessed is within a range of +/- 3 (for year and titles only). 🟡 for Country, indicates a match in the Continent.

(v) For eg: a 🟢 in the first position (Year), indicates that the player you entered has won some GS in the same year as in question. A 🔴 in the first position (Year), indicates that the player you entered has not won any GS in the year as in question. It DOES NOT mean that the player your entered and the mystery player has not won a GS in the same year. Remember, only ONE win of the mystery player is being compared here.

(vi) Similarly, a 🔴 in the second position (Slam), indicates that the person you entered has not won the particular slam in question. For eg. if your player has won the FO and the USO, and you get a 🔴 for the second position (Slam), it simply means that this particular Win of the mystery player is either the WIM or AO. They might (or not) have won the FO or the USO at a different time in their career.

(vii) Switch to the EASY Mode any time during the game, where the attributes are revealed, as shown below, after each INCORRECT guess. The clues are more direct in this Mode.


(viii) On your Final Try, after you have enabled the Easy Mode, you have the option to look at an ADDITIONAL HINT. This is an absolute give-away 😀!

(ix) A new feature of awarding RANKING POINTS has been introduced. Depending on which attempt you won the game at, you will be awarded points. It starts at 10 points for the first attempt and reduces by a point for each subsequent try. If you switch to the Easy Mode you will be docked 2 points and if you use the Additional Hint a further 2 points will be docked. No points are awarded if you fail to identify the player. TIER is determined based on Total Points. Try and get to GRAND SLAM level!
